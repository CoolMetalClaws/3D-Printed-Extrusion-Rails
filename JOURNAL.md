# Engineering Journal for ModuRail: A Modular 3D Printed 2020 V-Slot Extrusion Rail System

## Problem: 

I required extrusion rails for a project, and all available ones were expensive and with long shipping times. I decided that printing them would be a more cost effective and easy way to use them for my application.

## Day 1 (2hrs 13mins) : 

I Fully sketched out and extruded the end face of an extrusion rail using an official orthogonal sketch with dimensions. This ensured accuracy and compatability with cmmercially sold extrusion rails. The end face was extremely complicated to sketch and took a while, but the end result has accurate dimensions. I began to sketch the sliders for the rails with an exact fit.

## Day 2 (1hr 46mins) :

I completed sketching and extruding the sliders, the feet, and the connectors. All parts were labelled for clarity and organisation, as all the tabs were confusing to keep a track of. To make the end peice, i simply duplicated the sliders 4 times around the same axis. Then, i sketched a base for them all to connect to, completing all the parts.

## Day 3 (1hr 32mins) : 

I 3D printed all nessecary sections required to test the fitting between parts. Unfortunately none of the parts fit, as i realised i forgot to account for PLA shrinkage. I altered all parts to have a 0.2 mm tolerance, which is universal for most printers. After reprinting all parts, the feet were too loose and kept sliding around, but they were meant to be stationary. I adjusted the feet print tolerances to 0.12mm, making it perfectly tight.
